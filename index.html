About the Program

What is the OSSD?

The Ontario Secondary School Diploma (OSSD) is the official high school graduation credential issued by the Province of Ontario, Canada — one of the most respected educational systems in the world.

Issued under the authority of the Ontario Ministry of Education, the OSSD is recognized by universities and colleges across Canada, the United States, the United Kingdom, Australia, and many other countries worldwide.

At ICEC, we deliver the OSSD program through a flexible online format specifically designed to meet the needs of international students — allowing you to study at your own pace while meeting rigorous Canadian academic standards.

Completing the OSSD opens doors to prestigious post-secondary institutions globally and gives you the English-language academic foundation employers and universities demand.

Program Structure

How the Program is Structured

The OSSD requires 30 credits and other graduation requirements, typically completed in 1–2 years depending on your prior education.

📚

Compulsory Credits

18 compulsory credits are required, covering core academic disciplines:

  • English (4 credits)
  • Mathematics (3 credits)
  • Sciences (2 credits)
  • Canadian History & Geography (2 credits)
  • Arts, Health & Physical Education
  • French as a Second Language (1 credit)
  • Career Studies & Civics (1 credit)
🎨

Elective Credits

12 elective credits allow you to customize your diploma based on your university goals and career interests:

  • Business Studies
  • Computer Science & Technology
  • Social Sciences & Humanities
  • Additional Sciences & Maths
  • Arts & Communications
  • Cooperative Education
✅

Graduation Requirements

Beyond the 30 credits, students must fulfill:

  • Ontario Secondary School Literacy Test (OSSLT)
  • 40 hours of Community Involvement
  • At least one Grade 11 or 12 course in English
  • Minimum Grade 12 level in subject areas required by your chosen university

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to enroll in the OSSD program?
The ICEC OSSD program is open to international students who have completed at least Grade 9 or equivalent in their home country. Our academic advisors will review your transcripts to determine your eligibility and assess which credits you may be granted based on prior learning.
How long does it take to complete the OSSD?
Completion time varies based on your existing education and the number of credits required. Most students complete the program in 1 to 2 years. Your ICEC academic advisor will help you build a realistic timeline based on your specific situation and goals.
Are ICEC courses taught by certified teachers?
Yes. All courses at ICEC are taught by certified Ontario teachers who are fully qualified to deliver Ontario curriculum courses. They provide ongoing guidance, feedback, and academic support throughout your studies.
Can prior learning be credited?
Yes. Through Prior Learning Assessment and Recognition (PLAR), students may receive credit for knowledge and skills gained through previous study. Our advisors will review your existing transcripts to determine applicable credits, potentially reducing your time to graduation.
Is the OSSD accepted for Canadian university admission?
Absolutely. The OSSD is the standard high school graduation credential in Ontario and is accepted by all Canadian universities and colleges. It is also recognized by hundreds of international institutions in the US, UK, Australia, and worldwide.
What is the Ontario Secondary School Literacy Test (OSSLT)?
The OSSLT is a mandatory graduation requirement that assesses students' reading and writing skills in English. ICEC prepares all students for the OSSLT with dedicated support materials and practice resources to ensure you are fully ready on test day.
How do I get started?
Start by booking a free consultation with one of our academic advisors. We'll review your background, explain the program in detail, and create a customized pathway plan for you. Click "Book a Free Consultation" at the top of this page to get started.
